## 🔍 Deal Deep-Dive: AI's $662B Lease Liability Problem

**The story no one's talking about:** According to a GlobeSt analysis published February 27, the major AI/hyperscaler companies — Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, and Oracle — have collectively committed to an estimated **$662 billion in future lease obligations** tied to data center and campus expansions.

**Why CRE investors should care:**

- **Concentration risk is extreme.** A single sector (AI infrastructure) now represents the largest tenant class in industrial/data center CRE. If AI investment cycles decelerate — as Nvidia's stock volatility suggests markets are beginning to price in — the knock-on effects for landlords could be severe.

- **Lease structures are unusually long.** Many of these commitments are 15–20 year triple-net leases with limited termination options, which benefits landlords on paper. But the obligations are often structured through special-purpose entities with limited recourse to the parent company's balance sheet.

- **Geographic concentration amplifies risk.** Northern Virginia (Loudoun County), central Ohio, and the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex account for over 60% of these commitments. A correction in AI capex would disproportionately impact these markets.

- **The bull case:** These leases are backed by the most cash-rich companies in human history. Microsoft alone has $75B+ in cash reserves. The risk of default is low — but the risk of *growth deceleration* in new commitments is real, which would slow the development pipeline and compress land values in key data center corridors.

**Our take:** The AI data center build-out is the single most important demand driver in CRE today. But prudent investors should be stress-testing portfolios against a scenario where AI capex moderates by 30–40% from current run rates.

## 🌡️ Market Sentiment

**Bullish signals:**
- Transaction volume up 31% YoY — strongest February since 2022
- Fed signaling 2 more rate cuts in 2026
- CMBS delinquency rate declined to 4.8% in January (from 5.3% peak in mid-2025)
- Private credit funds have raised $48B for CRE deployment in 2026

**Bearish signals:**
- Office-to-residential conversion pipeline (~45M SF nationally) will add supply pressure to both sectors
- Insurance costs continue rising 15–25% annually in coastal/disaster-prone markets
- Multifamily supply deliveries peaking in Q1–Q2 2026 (~150,000 units nationally)
- Remote work adoption has stabilized at ~28% of work days (Stanford WFH Research), capping office recovery

**Net assessment: Cautiously bullish.** The rate cycle is doing the heavy lifting, but fundamentals are improving unevenly. Industrial and life sciences remain the strongest conviction sectors. Office requires extreme selectivity. Multifamily is a 2027 story once the supply wave passes.
